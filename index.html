Case Study on Workplace Conflict Across Cultures

A documented narrative of cross-cultural miscommunication between an American and Japanese product team. Explores how communication styles — shaped by cultural values — create professional friction even when both parties act in good faith, and how empathy and structural change can resolve it.

Lost in Noise — Reworking a Cluttered Advertisement

A critical deconstruction of the Fair & Lovely advertising campaign, identifying ethical failures in visual and verbal communication — colorism, false product claims, and harmful messaging — followed by a redesigned ad that communicates the same product honestly, without exploiting social insecurity.

03 ✦ Oral / Aural Mode

How Social Media Has Changed the Way We Communicate

A formal persuasive speech examining social media's transformative — and often corrosive — effect on human communication. The speech argues that while social media has democratised access and amplified voices, it has traded depth for speed, leaving us connected in quantity but impoverished in quality.
